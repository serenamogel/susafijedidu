Kevin Nash Age, Height, Weight

Kevin Nash Age, Height, Weight: Kevin Nash, officially known as Kevin Scott Nash was born on July 9, 1959, in Detroit, Michigan, United States.

He’s an American actor, podcaster, and retired professional wrestler, currently signed to World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) under a Legends contract.

Nash is best known for his tenures with World Championship Wrestling (WCW) and Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (TNA).

He became famous in the professional wrestling world by performing for World Championship Wrestling (WCW) from 1990–1993 under multiple ring names

In 1993, he signed with the World Wrestling Federation (WWF), now World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) under the name Diesel, nicknamed “Big Daddy Cool”.

As Diesel, he achieved status as a top star in the company, winning the WWF World, Intercontinental, and Tag Team Championships (the WWF Triple Crown).

While in the WWF, Nash was part of The Kliq, an influential backstage group that included Shawn Michaels, Triple H, Scott Hall, and Sean Waltman.

He exited WWF and returned to WCW in 1996, where he performed under his real name, partnering with Hall as the Outsiders and founding the nWo with Hulk Hogan

He maintained his status as a top star, winning the WCW World Heavyweight Championship five times and the WCW World Tag Team Championship nine times

After WCW left the business, he returned to the WWF where he briefly reformed the nWo and feuded with Triple H over the World Heavyweight Championship.

Nash then performed in Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (TNA) for seven years, and he held their Legends and Tag Team championships once each

In 2011, he returned to World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) under a legends contract and he continues to make sporadic appearances for the promotion.

During his in-ring career, Nash won 20 major championships, including six world championships and twelve world tag team championships.

He’s also a two-time WWE Hall of Famer. Nash was individually inducted into the class of 2015 and in 2020 as a nWo member alongside Hogan, Hall, and Waltman.

He headlined numerous pay-per-view events for WCW, WWF/WWE, and TNA, including WCW’s flagship event, Starrcade, where he ended Bill Goldberg’s undefeated streak.
